Healthcare facilities, particularly in resource-constrained environments, face significant challenges in ensuring timely and accurate medication delivery due to staff overload, missed doses, and human error. This project presents a low-cost, modular, AI-powered medical assistant robot designed to automate medication dispensing and improve patient safety. The system integrates a Raspberry Pi-based face recognition module for patient identification with a time-scheduled pill dispensing mechanism, ensuring personalized and accurate medication delivery. It employs line-following navigation using infrared sensors, making it suitable for structured indoor environments such as hospitals and elderly care centers. The robot features a three-compartment modular architecture consisting of a mobility unit, storage unit, and an AI-enabled dispensing unit, allowing scalability and ease of maintenance. Additionally, the system supports real-time monitoring, image-based logging, and backend communication through wireless connectivity. By combining artificial intelligence, embedded systems, and web technologies, the proposed system provides an efficient, reliable, and scalable solution for smart healthcare automation.
